I am a mathematician interested in exploring biochemical reaction networks and the geometry and topology of multi-scale biological systems.


I am a Van Loo Independent Postdoctoral Fellowship at the University of Michigan Ann Arbor, co-hosted by the Michigan Center for Applied and Interdisciplinary Mathematics and Center for the Study of Complex Systems. I completed my PhD in Systems Biology PhD at Harvard University advised by Jeremy Gunawardena. My doctoral work explores graph-theoretic models of biochemical reaction networks, primarily through the linear framework. My background in graph theory and stochastic processes allows me to investigate open problems across disciplines, ranging from non-equilibrium thermodynamics to molecular information processing. I also have done work in chemical reaction network theory with Polly Yu on disguised toric systems, which is published in SIAM Journal of Dynamical Systems. I graduated from Middlebury College in 2018 with a B.A. in Mathematics and Biochemistry.
